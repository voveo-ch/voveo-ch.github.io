Features

End-to-end process

Onboarding

As a participant of an event, you just enter your access code. After the host accredits you, you're ready and in. There is no user/password or seed phrase you need to write down or remember. It just takes a few seconds.

Hosting

Super simple administration. As an admin of an event or assembly, you easily accredit participants and set up the agenda.

Accreditation

As a host of an event, you create an invitation code per participants which they will then use to accredit. This way you make sure no user signs on twice or any unwanted 3rd party is joining.

Disposable

We provide you with a secure yet disposable account. When using Voveo, we create a private/public keypair for you which is stored in your local browser. After an event, you decide whether you want to add this key to your wallet or just dispose it.

Usability

Video

Run a video feed during your event. Share your screen or presentation. Make a video conference call with all participants. We integrate open-source software Jitsi.

Chat

We offer video and text chat (integrated in Jitsi). This is seperated from the voting, which means that you can identify yourself on the video but still are private (aka pseudonymous) when you vote.

Minutes

After concluding an event, meeting minutes with the voting results are generated automatically and ready to be copied, printed or saved as PDF.

Storage

Stored on the blockchain forever and immutable. Since all data is on the blockchain, there is no need to keep a record on our your server.

Technology

Ethereum mainnet

Run on the Ethereum mainnet, one of the most secure databases in the world.

Ethereum testnet

Voveo runs on Ethereum testnet, where it can be used for free. Be aware that the testnets do not have the same security as the mainnet.

Ethereum PoA

Coming soon: Run on Proof of Authority blockchain. Fast, efficient and cheaper than the Ethereum mainnet.

Security

Whatever blockchain you choose, your event is secure. Your actions are signed with a private key that only exists on your local machine in the browser. Everything you do is securely deployed and stored in the blockchain.

FAQ

Why are results written to the blockchain?

The blockchain is a secure and immutable data record.

The blockchain is public, so is everything I do on Voveo public?

Basically yes. This means you should be careful of what you do with Voveo. However, as long as you do not share any personal information, you are pseudonymous - there is a track record of your account, but no one knows which person this account belongs to.

How am I identified?

The administrator hands out a personalized access code that you should not share with anybody

How can I revert/undo my vote/choice?

You can't. That is why there is a confirmation. You may of course ask the administrator to invalidate the ballot and to repeat the vote.

What blockchain do you use?

Voveo runs on Ethereum and is connected to the Ethereum mainnet. However, we provide access to other Ethereum-based blockchain, e.g. Ropsten testnet, PoA or your custom blockchain

Voting should be confidential. Can anyone see what I voted for?

Your vote is treated confidentially. The blockchain records a) who has voted and b) how many yes/no-votes are cast - there is no record of what you have voted for.

What are these cryptic strings, e.g. 0x08dc456423...?

Since everything runs on the blockchain, we use smart contracts. Every smart contracts has such an identifier. Your public key too is such a string. Don't worry, there is no need for you to remember or write down these strings.
